Body Movements in Movement Education

Flexion – bending a joint, decreasing the angle between body parts. Example: bending the elbow when lifting weights.

Extension – straightening a joint, increasing the angle between body parts. Example: straightening the knee when standing up.

Abduction – moving a body part away from the midline. Example: raising the arm sideways.

Adduction – moving a body part toward the midline. Example: bringing the arm back down to the side.

Go to the video

_________________________________________

Hyperflexion – bending a joint beyond its normal limit. Example: bending the neck too far forward.

Hyperextension – straightening a joint beyond its normal range. Example: pushing the knee backward past straight.

_________________________________________

Rotation – pag-ikot ng buto o bahagi ng katawan sa sariling axis.

Medial (Internal) Rotation – pag-ikot papasok, patungo sa gitna ng katawan.

Lateral (External) Rotation – pag-ikot palabas, palayo sa gitna ng katawan.

Elevation – lifting a body part upward. Example: shrugging the shoulders up.

Depression – moving a body part downward. Example: lowering the shoulders back down.

Lateral Flexion – bending the body sideways at the waist or neck. Example: tilting the head toward the shoulder.

_________________________________________

Circumduction – moving a limb in a circular motion. Example: making arm circles.

_________________________________________

Inversion – turning the sole of the foot inward. Example: twisting the foot so the sole faces the other foot.

Eversion – turning the sole of the foot outward. Example: twisting the foot so the sole faces away from the other foot.
